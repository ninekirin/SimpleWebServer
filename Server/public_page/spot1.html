The last tear in the Atlantic Ocean --- Sayram Lake

Introduction

Sayram Lake is located in Bortala Mongol Autonomous Prefecture, Xinjiang. The longest lake is 29.6 kilometers from east to west, and the widest lake is 25.7 kilometers from north to south.

Historical Evolution

The origin of the name of "Sayram Lake": The local people call Sayram Lake Santai Haizi. It was named (Santai) due to the fact that there was a military station of Elzhe Itubor on the east bank of the lake in the Qing Dynasty.

Geographic Environment

Sayram Lake is located in the western section of Tianshan Mountains, the southwest end of the Junggar Basin. The lake area is located at 80 ° 59 ′ 35 ″~81 ° 22 ′ 17.5 ″ E, 44 ° 29 ′ 37.4 ″~44 ° 32 ′ 19 ″ N. The elevation of Sayram Lake is 2073m, the lake area is 458k ㎡, and the lake supply coefficient is about 3.1, which is the smallest of the inland lakes in northwest China.

Natural Resources

  • Animal resources

    • There are 19 kinds of plankton in Sayram Lake, including 6 kinds of protozoa, accounting for 36.1%.

  • Fish resources

    • In 1998, Xinjiang introduced cold water fish such as high white salmon and concave order white salmon from Russia for aquaculture

Tourist Information

Best viewing time:

  • December to March - Blue Ice Puzzle Wonder
  • April to May - Ice melts and can be pushed ashore during strong winds
  • June to August - The wild flowers in the lake area are in full bloom, reflecting the blue waters of the snowy mountains
